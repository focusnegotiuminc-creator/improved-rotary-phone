# Payroll and Earned Wage Access Options Memo

Prepared: 2026-03-27

This memo is a practical operations note, not financial, tax, or legal advice. Product terms can change, so confirm inside the provider account before enrolling.

## Short Answer

If you need the fastest realistic path from this workspace, **Square Payroll** appears to be the most actionable option from the official sources reviewed because:

- it supports direct deposit and contractor payroll,
- it documents faster payroll options,
- it documents instant payments for team members under specific conditions,
- it already overlaps with your website payment ecosystem if you want Square on the site too.

## What The Official Sources Show

### Square Payroll

Official sources reviewed:

- Square contractor payroll pricing:
  - https://squareup.com/us/en/payroll/contractors
- Square instant payments support article:
  - https://squareup.com/help/us/en/article/6917-send-instant-payments-with-square-payroll
- Square two-day payroll support article:
  - https://squareup.com/help/us/en/article/6456-two-day-payroll
- Square payroll legal terms:
  - https://squareup.com/us/en/legal/general/payroll

Key takeaways from those pages:

- Contractor-only Square Payroll is advertised at **$6 per contractor paid per month**.
- Square says contractor payroll supports direct deposit and repeated contractor payments.
- For **full-service Square Payroll subscribers**, Square documents an **instant payments** option.
- Square says that when using **Square Balance** as the payment source, team members paid by direct deposit can receive pay on the **next business day**, and team members using **Cash App** can receive pay **instantly**, including weekends.
- Square also documents a **two-day payroll** feature, but approval can take **up to 2 business days**.

Operational reading:

- If Sierra is treated as a W-2 employee, contractor-only payroll is not the right path.
- If you need a same-day or near-same-day employee access path, Square’s own instant-pay documentation is the strongest current official source I found.
- This still requires employer onboarding, company/tax setup, employee onboarding, and a funded payment source.

### Gusto

Official source reviewed:

- https://gusto.com/pricing

Key takeaways from that page:

- Simple plan: **$49/mo + $6/mo per person**
- Plus plan: **$80/mo + $12/mo per person**
- Premium plan: **$180/mo + $22/mo per person**
- Next-day pay add-on: **$15/mo + $3/mo per person**
- Same-day pay: **$90 per payroll**
- Instant pay: **$100 per payroll**

Operational reading:

- Gusto is not a free payroll path.
- It is strong for payroll compliance, but it is not the cheapest if your priority is immediate access and low setup cost.

### Branch

Official sources reviewed:

- https://www.branchapp.com/solutions/earned-wage-access
- https://my.branchapp.com/on-demand-pay

Key takeaways from those pages:

- Branch offers **earned wage access / on-demand pay**.
- Workers can access a **portion of earned wages ahead of payday** through the Branch app.
- Branch emphasizes access through a Branch account/card and also mentions ACH and instant transfer options in some flows.

Operational reading:

- Branch appears useful as an earned-wage-access layer, but the public pages reviewed do **not** present a simple self-serve payroll setup path like Square Payroll.
- Public pricing for employer setup was **not** clearly disclosed on the pages reviewed.
- Branch looks more like a payroll-adjacent worker access tool than a same-day standalone payroll launch tool for your exact use case.

### DailyPay

Official sources reviewed:

- https://www.dailypay.com/on-demand-pay-small-business/
- https://www.dailypay.com/workday/

Key takeaways from those pages:

- DailyPay positions itself as an **on-demand pay / earned wage access** solution.
- DailyPay emphasizes working **with existing payroll systems** rather than replacing payroll.
- DailyPay highlights integrations and partnerships instead of a simple standalone payroll account path.

Operational reading:

- DailyPay may be useful later, but it does **not** look like the fastest way to stand up payroll from scratch today.
- Public employer pricing was **not** clearly disclosed on the reviewed pages.

## Recommendation

### Best immediate path from the reviewed sources

1. **Square Payroll** for the fastest practical launch path.
2. Use **full-service Square Payroll** if the worker is a W-2 employee.
3. If you need faster employee access, evaluate **Square instant payments with Square Balance** after onboarding is complete.
4. Keep **Branch or DailyPay** as later-phase earned-wage-access evaluations, not the first implementation path.

## Important Limits

- I cannot tell you what credit limit you would be approved for.
- I did **not** find any official source promising you a lawful built-in employer funding float of **three weeks**.
- Payroll providers still require company setup, tax setup, worker classification, and funding.
- Because payroll and worker classification are high-stakes, final provider selection should be confirmed carefully before submission.
