# Madison County Case Workflow

This is a factual, pro se workflow draft for organizing a response. It is not legal advice, and it intentionally does not use sovereign-citizen, "secured party creditor," "maritime law," or UCC immunity arguments because those theories are generally not recognized by Illinois courts and can damage credibility.

## Immediate Goal

Ask for the current case status, preserve defenses, show proof of insurance if applicable, and request a hearing or relief using official Illinois and Madison County forms.

## Official Sources

- Madison County Circuit Court information and contact:
  - Main phone listed online: `(618) 296-4580`
  - Case-search path referenced by the court information site: `https://www.co.madison.il.us/departments/circuit_court/index.php`
- Illinois approved motion forms:
  - Motion and Notice forms: `https://www.illinoiscourts.gov/documents-and-forms/approved-forms/circuit-forms/circuit-forms-motion/`
- Madison County non-attorney motion packet:
  - `https://cms4files.revize.com/madisoncountyilus/document_center/Motion_Forms_for_Non_Attorneys.pdf`

## Evidence Packet to Assemble

1. Citation, ticket, complaint, or bench-judgment paperwork.
2. Filed continuance request and affidavit, plus proof of filing or transmission.
3. Proof of insurance covering the relevant date if available.
4. Any written communication with the clerk, prosecutor, or court.
5. A short factual timeline explaining:
   - why you did not appear,
   - what happened with the vehicle,
   - what happened regarding insurance,
   - what action you took afterward.
6. Any records showing the electric vehicle failed and could not be moved immediately.

## Recommended Factual Workflow

1. Confirm the exact case number, charge names, court location, and present status through the official case search or clerk.
2. Ask the clerk what motion the court expects for your current posture:
   - motion to continue,
   - motion to vacate/set aside a bench judgment,
   - motion for status hearing,
   - proof-of-insurance filing.
3. Use the official Illinois or Madison County motion form instead of a homemade format unless the clerk instructs otherwise.
4. Keep every argument factual and tied to documents:
   - continuance was filed,
   - nonappearance was not intentional,
   - proof of insurance exists if true,
   - vehicle circumstances prevented immediate movement if true,
   - you are requesting a hearing to address the merits.
5. File the motion with the clerk and mail or deliver the required copy to the other side as the form instructions require.
6. Save a stamped copy and proof of service.
7. Calendar the next hearing date immediately after filing.

## Arguments To Avoid

- "Supreme law of the land" exemption from traffic enforcement
- "Maritime law" objections
- "Secured party creditor" immunity
- "Natural person traveling" as a complete defense to licensing or insurance laws
- Any theory that a judgment is void merely because there was "no harm"
